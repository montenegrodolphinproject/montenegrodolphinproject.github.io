Our call for speakers is open till 10 december, 2017. Thank you to everyone who put in the time and effort to submit their ideas! We will be in touch soon with the outcome of your submissions.

Instructions on Submitting Abstracts

Deadline for submission of abstracts: 1 November 2017 (23:00 GMT)

Only one presentation will be accepted per presenting author First authors must be registered by the late registration deadline (1 December 2017) Short talks and posters can be presented by the co-authors

Abstract title: Provide the title of the abstract, no more than 20 words.

Authors’ names: Provide the authors’ names, surnames and Institutes/affiliations.

Abstract: Abstract should be one continuous paragraph., with a word limit of 250. The abstract should be uploaded in MS WORD format and PdF wouldn’t be accepted.

Presentation type: Three presentation type will be accepted; - long talk - short talk - poster presentation with all having the same importance.

All Abstracts will be subject to anonymous review and selected on merit. The form of presentation is not a reflection of the value of the research.

Presentation slight submission: All approved oral talks should email their presentation slight to…… no later than 30 January 2018

Information about presentation type

Long talks

Long talks will be 20 minutes in total, followed by 5 minutes questions. The presenter will be notified when five minute is left.

Short talks

Short talk will be 5 minutes in total, followed by 2 minutes questions. This format suits suit ideas or hypothesis, innovative methodology and even best and worst field experiences they want to share. There shouldn’t be more than 10 slights. The presenter will be notified when one minute is left.

Posters

Size: Please keep a maximum poster size (A0 format) - 85cm (width) x 120cm (height).

Poster must include a photo of the PRESENTING author (with surname) at the top left corner

The title: Not less than 96pt

The conclusions: Not less than 72 pt

Text: No lettering less than 30pt if you want people to read it!

Graphs and figures are more effective ways of transferring information than tables or text.

Avoid use of copyrighted images unless you are the copyright holder or have permission to use them.

A4 prints to be taken by participants: it is useful to bring a number of color or black and white prints in the A4 format to be displayed close to your poster, for participants to take it back home. Make sure you have enough copies for the duration of the conference.
